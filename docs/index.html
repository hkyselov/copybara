What it does

Small app, long memory

  • History that keeps up

    Plain text, rich text, and images are captured the moment you copy them.

  • Paste on select

    Pick a clip and it lands straight in the app you're working in — no extra ⌘V.

  • Pin what matters

    Keep the clips you reuse at hand; remove the rest, or Clear All for a fresh start.

  • Clip details on demand

    Inspect any history item — full contents, type, and size — in its own window.

  • Out of your way

    No dock icon. The popup auto-hides when you're done and remembers its spot on every display.

  • Fits your setup

    Light, dark, or system appearance. Adjustable font size and preview lines, open at login, your own shortcut.

Install

On your Mac in a minute

Fastest: Homebrew

One command, and --no-quarantine skips the unsigned-app warning entirely.

brew install --cask hkyselov/tap/copybara --no-quarantine
  1. Download the .dmg from the latest release — universal, for Intel and Apple Silicon.
  2. Drag Copybara into Applications and launch it from the menu bar.
  3. Grant Accessibility permission when macOS asks — that's what lets Copybara paste a clip into the active app.

First launch on an unsigned app

Copybara is a free project without a paid Apple developer account, so macOS warns on first open. Click Done, then allow it under System Settings → Privacy & Security → Open Anyway.

Prefer the terminal? Clear the quarantine flag instead:

xattr -cr /Applications/Copybara.app
